Campaign 01
Nike
DON'T LOSE ANOTHER
Courage to Indict
The Insight

80% of girls drop out of sport by age 14. Not because they want to. Because the world tells them they don't belong. Girls are hearing it through rosters, coaching culture, body shaming, media silence. And then it calls it a choice. This isn't attrition. It's exclusion.

The Strategy

This campaign is built around a wordplay system that embeds HER into the tagline. The iconic Swoosh is rendered in purple, signifying this cause as distinct and deliberate. Strong imagery of girls in sport stops viewers cold, exposes injustice, and leaves something impossible to ignore.

Campaign 02
Rivian
Rules Rewritten
Courage to Declare
The Insight

Electric vehicles have spent a decade selling restraint to the people who were already declaring the future. No electric vehicle has been bold enough to speak their language. Electric speaks to conscience. But it has never spoken to command until now.

The Strategy

The strategy reframes EVs from conscience to command. Darkness speaks directly to the audience's world of dark mode and premium aesthetics, where serious work happens. The headlights are the campaign's visual metaphor of forward dominance and seeing further than anyone else.

Campaign 03
L'Oréal
The Red Velvet Chair
Courage to Confront
The Insight

Beauty campaigns have spoken to aspiration while quietly gatekeeping worth. A self-aware, performance-fatigued Gen Z finds L'Oréal's original tagline "I'm worth it" increasingly hollow. A once radical slogan that had once promised empowerment has surrendered its radical core.

The Strategy

This campaign restores the radical core of "I'm worth it" through raw vulnerability and honest storytelling. The chair invites those the industry ignored to share their stories. Worth is no longer defined by performative beauty. Beauty is defined by the courage to be seen.

Campaign 04
LaCroix
"Your Summer Refresh"
Courage to Play
The Insight

People are trading sugary sodas for healthier options. But healthy drinks are too often branded as serious and functional. Summer gatherings deserve more than a compromise. They deserve a drink as bright, bold, and alive as the season itself.

The Strategy

Bright summer colors, repeated summer imagery, and wit-driven taglines turn this campaign into a vibrant visual celebration of summer. LaCroix becomes a fun, playful brand that looks, feels, and belongs exactly like the summer everyone wants.

Campaign 05
Breeze Airways
"See? Easy."
Courage to Simplify
The Insight

Flying has become something people endure rather than enjoy. Indirect routes, middle seats, and baggage chaos have normalized what passengers have simply accepted as the unavoidable cost of air travel. What was once the thrill of going somewhere has quietly become something to survive.

The Strategy

Humorous headlines and fun cartoon illustrations make the viewer laugh at the chaos of flying. The campaign doesn't lecture or promise luxury. It quietly points at the friction, disarms it with humor, and positions Breeze as the effortless solution to an experience that never had to be this hard.

Campaign 06
Pepsi
"It Was Theirs. Now It's Yours."
Courage to Claim
The Insight

Gen Z reinvents nostalgia culture as a native act, sampling the past to create the present. Their relationship to the 80s isn't memory but an aesthetic longing. Drawn to the texture, feeling, and energy of an era they never lived, they claim it, remix it, and make it entirely their own.

The Strategy

Bold vector illustrations and vibrant photography work together to build the visual world. The photography roots this campaign in the nostalgic warmth of the 80s, while graphic illustrations bring it forward with authentic Gen Z energy, to reference the era without recreating it.
